---
description: "Universal unstick — pause, re-ground, offer next moves"
---

**First, say to the learner:**

> "All good — let's slow down for a sec."

**Then:**

1. Read `shared-context/workshop-rules.md` — voice rules (especially: never imply learner fault).

2. Find the most recent `workshop-log.md` in `lesson-modules/W*/`. Read the last 5-10 entries.

3. Recap where they are in 1-2 sentences from the log: what workshop, what step, what was last decided.

4. Ask:
   > "What's feeling off? Pick one if it fits, or tell me in your own words:
   > (a) I'm not sure what to type
   > (b) something errored and I don't get it
   > (c) I lost the thread of what we're doing
   > (d) something else"

5. Branch on their answer:
   - **(a)** → re-prompt the exact next thing they should type, with one-line context
   - **(b)** → read the error message together, explain in plain language, suggest the fix
   - **(c)** → re-ground in scenario — Lina, Friday, what we're aiming for — in plain English
   - **(d)** → open dialogue; one clarifying question, then suggest a concrete next move

6. Append `## Stuck moment` to the workshop log with what was stuck and what unstuck it.

7. **Default attribution: the script or explanation failed them.** Mirror with phrasing like *"this part is one of the rougher ones"* or *"let me try saying it differently."* Never imply learner is slow / missing something / did it wrong.

8. Once unstuck, return to the workshop where they were.
