description: "Checkpoint Workshop 1-2 — write summary, acknowledge progress"
---

**First, say to the learner:**

> "Wrapping up W1-2. One sec — let me look back through what we did."

**Then:**

1. Read `shared-context/workshop-rules.md` (voice + §10 journaling/done behavior).

2. Read `lesson-modules/W1/1-2-linas-coffee/workshop-log.md`.

3. Classify the state by what's actually in the log:
   - **Completed:** all major Step entries present + success-point marker + bonus chapters done
   - **Checkpoint-completed:** success point hit, mid-bonus
   - **Checkpoint-incomplete:** success point not hit, but real progress made
   - **Aborted:** very early stop

4. Append a `## Summary` block to the log with:
   - Status (one of the four above)
   - Real progress in 3-5 specific bullets — name the actual decisions, files, insights. Never generic.
   - Where they left off, in concrete terms (never "we ran out of time" — say "the X file is the same shape, ~5 min when you come back")
   - Files produced (with paths)
   - Closed-at timestamp

5. Update the log frontmatter `status:` field to `checkpointed` (or `completed` if all bonus done).

6. Speak to the learner using voice rules. Lead with brief affirmation OK ("Great"/"Nice"), then **specific named progress** — what they actually did. Acknowledge effort warmly even if incomplete. 3-4 sentences. Examples:
   - *"Great — quick wrap on what we got done. Schema is locked, four of the six files are merged, and we already caught the Highland Beans typos. The last two are the same shape, so when you come back to this it's a 5-minute lift."*
   - *"Nice. You designed the schema, picked categories, ran the merge, surfaced the fixed-floor insight, and wrote Lina something she can actually send. Honestly the hard part is the schema — the rest is the same shape every time."*

7. **Offer reflection** (skippable):
   > "Two questions if you've got 3 minutes — totally skippable: what surprised you in the data? what would you do differently next time?"
   If they answer, append to log under `## Reflection`.

8. Tell them what's next:
   > "Next up is W1-3 — building the dashboard. Type `/start-1-3` whenever you're ready. Or take the break — your work is saved."

9. **Do NOT close the conversation.** If the learner keeps working, new entries go under a `## Post-done exploration` section in the log. The summary stands.
